About Me

I am an Ph.D. Candidate in the School of Informatics at the University of Edinburgh, supervised by Dr. Chris Xiaoxuan Lu. My sponsor source is the School of Informatics funded CDT-RAS scholarship. Prior to this, I received my B.Eng degree with the highest honored Academic Star from Tongji University in Shanghai (2017-2021). During my bachelor study, I worked as a Research Assistant at the Vision4Robotics Lab mentored by Dr. Changhong Fu and did research at the Tsinghua University as a visiting student.

My research interests lie in i) robust perception for mobile autonomy under adverse conditions, which focuses on developing scene understanding algorithms based on emerging 4D automotive radar; ii) weakly (self-) supervised learning of deep neural network for robot 3D vision. My previous research includes visual tracking, UAV localization and adversarial attack.

News

One paper accepted to IROS'22/RA-L

Our paper "Self-Supervised Scene Flow Estimation with 4-D Automotive Radar" is accepted by both IEEE RA-L and IROS'22. We propose a first-of-its-kind work to investigate the scene flow estimation from 4-D radar data. A self-supervised learning framework is proposed for scene flow estimation without resorting to costly annotations.
June 30, 2022
